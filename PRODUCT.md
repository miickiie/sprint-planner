# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Sprint Planner is for engineering managers and tech leads who need to explain sprint plans to stakeholders without turning planning into a heavyweight process. They work from Google Sheets as the source of truth, then use the app to make schedule, timing, and risk visible in a timeline that is easier to review.

## Product Purpose

The product turns sprint data from Google Sheets into a stakeholder-ready planning surface. It exists so tech leads can communicate what is planned, when work is expected to land, and where schedule risk may need attention while still keeping the underlying spreadsheet authoritative.

Success means stakeholders understand the plan, timing, and risk quickly, and teams keep Google Sheets as their source of truth without losing visual clarity.

## Positioning

Make sprint planning easier to explain, review, and adjust with stakeholders.

## Brand Personality

Fast, focused, and collaborative. The product should feel direct and practical in planning moments, with enough structure to support decision-making and enough lightness to stay approachable during stakeholder review.

## Anti-references

Sprint Planner should not feel like a heavy enterprise planning suite or a generic Jira clone. It should avoid bloated process language, dense configuration surfaces, and issue-tracker conventions that obscure the schedule instead of clarifying it.

## Design Principles

1. Make the schedule explain itself.
2. Keep Sheets visible as the source of truth.
3. Prioritize fast review over heavy configuration.
4. Make plan changes feel collaborative, not bureaucratic.
5. Surface risk through clarity, not alarm.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

This is currently an internal tool with no formal accessibility target. Future work should still preserve practical accessibility expectations for planning software: keyboard-friendly controls, readable contrast, clear sync state, and reduced-motion support.
